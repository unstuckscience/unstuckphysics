<p class="lead" markdown="1" style="border-left: 4px solid #2A52BE; padding-left: 1rem;">
Materials are classified by how easily they allow electrons to move through them. **Conductors** let them flow freely, while **insulators** hold onto them tightly.
</p>

## **Comparing Materials**

| Property | Conductors | Insulators |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Electron Bond** | Loose / "Sea of Electrons" | Tightly bound to nuclei |
| **Charge Flow** | Moves easily across surface | Remains localized where placed |
| **Examples** | Copper, Gold, Aluminum, Tap Water | Rubber, Glass, Plastic, Dry Wood |

{% capture cond_question %}Why is a metal wire usually wrapped in plastic?{% endcapture %}
{% capture cond_answer %}The metal is a **conductor** used to transport electricity efficiently. The plastic is an **insulator** used to prevent the charge from escaping the wire and causing a short circuit or giving someone a shock.{% endcapture %}
